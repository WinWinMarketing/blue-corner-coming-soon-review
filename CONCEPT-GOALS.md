# Blue Corner coming-soon — concept goals

## Shared goal

Replace the generic coming-soon page with a credible, emotionally direct first encounter for Canadian men considering support. Every direction carries the same locked story, tactile abstract material imagery rooted in the Blue Corner metaphor, local-only form demonstration, crisis guidance, supplied identity, and official palette: blue `#197CE3`, navy `#042874`, yellow `#EFC82C`, and off-white `#EFF3F9`.

## 01 — Held Seat

Make support feel available before a word is spoken. A worn wrap turning a reinforced blue corner and generous breathing room imply care held in reserve rather than a demand.

## 02 — Side by Side

Remove the pressure of confrontation. Folded wrap, torn paper, a faint ring mark, and open blue ground turn the space between rounds into a quiet reset without literal sports imagery.

## 03 — The Check-In

Build the experience around one direct question. Layered cloth surrounding an open off-white corner makes room for an answer while the short yellow stitch keeps the invitation precise.

## 04 — At the Corner

Use the boxing origin precisely but quietly: pressed concentric signals, a single yellow interruption, and worn blue canvas evoke the pause between rounds without turning mental health into sport or spectacle.

## 05 — The Long Walk

Frame opening up as forward movement. A worn padded corner held by one yellow stitch gives difficult copy room to breathe and presents progress as structure that can hold under pressure.

## 06 — Kitchen Light

Show the hour when the polished answer drops away. Warm window light crossing blue cloth and off-white paper gives the abstract system an after-hours intimacy without depicting a literal room.

## 07 — Workday Pause

Express the pressure of looking functional from the outside. Structured pacing, folded tape, a measured card, and a decisive yellow mark make the workday pause visible without dramatizing it.

## 08 — What Hands Say

Tell the story through small physical signals rather than staged expressions. Rope impressions crossing inked canvas make care, tension, and trust feel embedded in the material itself.

## 09 — Listening Room

Make therapy feel credible, calm, and approachable. A cyanotype of coiled rope and folded textile balances professional clarity with enough open paper to suggest room to speak honestly.

## 10 — Blue Margin

Treat the supplied copy as an editorial statement. A disciplined asymmetric grid and strong blue structural margin create authority without defaulting to corporate wellness design.

## 11 — On the Other End

Focus on the instant someone reaches out and someone answers. White cord paths meeting at a small yellow knot make connection feel immediate, protected, and low-pressure.

## 12 — Two Ways In

Give founding members and therapists equal clarity without splitting the brand in half. Layered fabric and torn-paper paths arrive at one small yellow junction, making the shared launch model easy to understand.

## Review recommendation

Start discussion with **03 — The Check-In** for directness, **06 — Kitchen Light** for emotional warmth, and **09 — Listening Room** for service credibility. Use the other nine to test how much metaphor, editorial structure, and material intimacy the brand should carry.

## Editorial gate

The `85%`, `#1`, and `~300%` statements are retained verbatim from the supplied reference page for design review. Verify each claim and link it to a primary source before any production launch.
