# Blue Corner coming-soon — concept goals

## Shared goal

Replace the generic coming-soon page with a credible, emotionally direct first encounter for Canadian men considering support. Every direction uses the same therapy-first story, verified Canadian evidence, crisis guidance, honest prototype form states, supplied Blue Corner identity, and official palette: blue `#197CE3`, navy `#042874`, yellow `#EFC82C`, and off-white `#EFF3F9`.

## 01 — Ringside Signal

Make the promise impossible to miss. High-contrast type and photographed ink-on-canvas material turn “Nobody fights alone” into a clear signal of strength without presenting therapy as a performance.

## 02 — Between Rounds

Create a considered editorial experience for someone pausing between difficult rounds. Torn paper, rope, measured numbering, and magazine-like pacing make the platform feel thoughtful, established, and human.

## 03 — The Open Corner

Lower the emotional temperature and make taking the next step feel safe. Generous space and a calm cyanotype crop frame therapy as an open invitation, not another demand.

## 04 — First Bell

Build urgency and launch energy without CGI or game styling. Fight-poster typography is grounded by a real canvas texture, exact brand colors, and direct therapy-first language.

## 05 — Someone in Your Corner

Lead with trust and human presence. Photographed cyanotype rope and hand-wrap forms create the most emotionally supportive direction: tactile, quiet, and believable.

## Recommendation

Use **05 — Someone in Your Corner** as the lead direction. It best balances the boxing metaphor with emotional safety. Keep **02 — Between Rounds** as the strongest editorial alternative and **01 — Ringside Signal** as the strongest high-impact alternative.
