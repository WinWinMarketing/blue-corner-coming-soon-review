# Blue Corner coming-soon — concept goals

## Shared goal

Replace the generic coming-soon page with a credible, emotionally direct first encounter for Canadian men considering support. Every direction carries the same locked story, realistic human imagery, local-only form demonstration, crisis guidance, supplied Blue Corner identity, and official palette: blue `#197CE3`, navy `#042874`, yellow `#EFC82C`, and off-white `#EFF3F9`.

## 01 — Held Seat

Make support feel available before a word is spoken. An intentionally open seat and generous breathing room turn the corner into a quiet invitation instead of a demand.

## 02 — Side by Side

Remove the pressure of a face-to-face confrontation. Parallel composition and shared ground suggest that someone can meet you where you are without asking you to perform.

## 03 — The Check-In

Build the experience around one direct, human question. Clear editorial hierarchy and close conversational imagery make honesty feel practical rather than clinical.

## 04 — At the Corner

Use the boxing origin precisely but quietly: the pause, the reset, and the person waiting between rounds. It carries the metaphor without turning mental health into sport or spectacle.

## 05 — The Long Walk

Frame opening up as forward movement. A natural outdoor conversation gives difficult copy room to breathe and presents progress as something taken one step at a time.

## 06 — Kitchen Light

Show the hour when the polished answer drops away. Warm domestic realism grounds the message in the ordinary places where honest conversations actually begin.

## 07 — Workday Pause

Meet the man who looks functional from the outside. Structured pacing, compressed workday details, and a decisive pause make hidden burnout visible without dramatizing it.

## 08 — What Hands Say

Tell the story through small physical signals rather than staged expressions. Close documentary crops make care, tension, and trust feel observed instead of performed.

## 09 — Listening Room

Make therapy feel credible, calm, and human. The most service-forward direction balances professional confidence with a room that feels warm enough to speak honestly.

## 10 — Blue Margin

Treat the supplied copy as an editorial statement. A disciplined asymmetric grid and strong blue structural margin create authority without defaulting to corporate wellness design.

## 11 — On the Other End

Focus on the instant someone reaches out and someone answers. Private phone imagery and a clear vertical path make the first step feel immediate and low-pressure.

## 12 — Two Ways In

Give founding members and therapists equal clarity without splitting the brand in half. The two conversion paths arrive at one shared corner and make the launch model easy to understand.

## Review recommendation

Start discussion with **03 — The Check-In** for directness, **06 — Kitchen Light** for emotional realism, and **09 — Listening Room** for service credibility. Use the other nine to test how much metaphor, editorial structure, and documentary intimacy the brand should carry.

## Editorial gate

The `85%`, `#1`, and `~300%` statements are retained verbatim from the supplied reference page for design review. Verify each claim and link it to a primary source before any production launch.
