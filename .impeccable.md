## Design Context

### Users
Blue Corner serves Canadian men who may be carrying burnout, isolation, anxiety, addiction, or crisis without naming it, plus therapists and founding partners who want to support them. The coming-soon experience must make guarded visitors feel recognized without exposing, diagnosing, or speaking down to them, then provide a calm route to early access.

### Brand Personality
Protective, direct, and quietly defiant. Blue Corner should feel like an editorial public-interest campaign with the steadiness of someone waiting between rounds: human, plain-spoken, credible, and never performatively clinical.

### Aesthetic Direction
Use the supplied reference as the master visual system: off-white editorial pages, dominant bright Blue Corner blue, dark navy ink, rare yellow signal bars, hard rules, oversized left-anchored typography, numbered evidence, asymmetric compositions, and intact approved corner assets. Boxing should appear through the metaphor of the corner, rope geometry, bell rhythm, canvas, tape, and held space—not fighters, sports-poster drama, games, glossy stock scenes, decorative gradients, or logo abuse.

### Design Principles
- Build six distinct editorial narratives from one unmistakable Blue Corner system.
- Preserve the reference page’s section rhythm, density, hierarchy, and sharp geometry.
- Use bright blue as the primary field; reserve navy for ink, text, and limited manifesto surfaces.
- Keep yellow rare and functional: dividers, numbers, active states, and primary action emphasis.
- Make every claim sourceable, every form state explicit, every control keyboard-accessible, and every layout stable from 320px through desktop.
